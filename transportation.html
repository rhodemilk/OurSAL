Transportation

How does transportation affect air pollution?

The transportation everyone uses on their daily commute to work, school, and other places does affect your carbon emissions. Per year, a standard gasoline vehicle emits about 4.6 metric tons of carbon dioxide per year. Vehicles, particularly those powered by fossil fuels such as gasoline and diesel, release greenhouse gasses such as carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and other pollutants into the atmosphere.

Trains

Trains are a sustainable mode of transport that helps reduce environmental impact, conserve resources, and improve mobility for both passengers and freight. They can carry a large number of passengers over long distances using relatively less carbon emissions per person. Trains also help combat traffic congestion and overall carbon emissions.

Brightline

A privately owned intercity train service for commuters in Florida. Brightline's South Florida train stations are strategically positioned near local transportation alternatives, allowing travelers easy access to the major destinations in each city. The brightline connects two cities Miami and Orlando in just over 3 hours.

Brightline Link

Tri-Rail

The Tri-Rail carries over fifteen thousand riders each day. Every year, a total of over 4.4 million persons ride the Tri-Rail. Tri-Rail operates along the CSX corridor and also runs shuttle service to connect with its stations. The Tri-Rail has stops throughout Palm Beach, Broward and Miami-Dade areas.

Tri-Rail Link

Amtrak Palmetto

Amtrak operates the Silver Service and Palmetto intercity passenger routes from Miami through Fort Lauderdale on the South Florida Rail Corridor north to New York City through Tampa and Orlando. The Fort Lauderdale station is located southwest of Broward Boulevard and I-95, beside Tri-Rail and BCT bus service. Amtrak serves intercity rail passengers at two additional stops in Hollywood and Deerfield Beach.

Amtrak Website

Buses

Buses provide easy ways to get around and are a great way to reduce your carbon footprint. Busses are also a great way to reduce the amount of pollution that is emitted by cars. You can find any bus near you with the Maps app on your phone. Buses provide you with a level of convenience that may not be available with cars, like saving money from buying gas, and you can also combat usual traffic congestion.

Miami MetroBuses

Metrobus serves over 95 routes, with around 1,000 buses traveling 29 million miles each year, 365 days a year. Miami-Dade County's Metrobus public bus system, which connects to Metrorail and Metromover. Metrobus routes connect sections of the city that Metrorail does not, such as Miami Beach, Key Biscayne, Homestead, and even Fort Lauderdale and the Florida Keys.

Metrobus Link

Broward County Transit

Broward County Transit (BCT) provides fixed route bus, express and community shuttles and paratransit – door-to-door – services in Broward County. The Broward County Transit operates 3 limited express (Breeze) and 33 local fixed routes within a 410 square mile service area using 291 fixed route buses.

BCT Link

Cars

There are different kinds of cars that are much better alternatives to the gas emmitting ones.

Electric Cars

The electric car is powered by a battery that is charged using electricity instead of gasoline or diesel. By running on electricity that can be generated from renewable sources such as solar or wind power, the electric car serves as a sustainable transportation option when compared to the regular carbon-emitting gasoline automobile.

Electric Link

Hybrid Cars

Hybrid cars are vehicles that use a combination of two or more power sources to run. Usually there is an internal combustion engine for gasoline and an electric motor for electricity. Hybrid cars can reduce fuel consumption resulting in lower greenhouse gas emissions and cheaper fuel costs for drivers.

Hybrid Link

Simple Alternatives

When you have a close destination, or need to do some exercise, it is best to not risk harming the environment for a two-minute drive. Instead, take these small distances down by simply walking or biking.

Bikes

Riding bikes is one of the least carbon emitting ways to travel instead of using other carbon-intensive modes of transportation. If your destination is bikeable, try increasing the number of times bike per week. Cycling is a great way to stay healthy and build endurance.

Bike Link

Walking

Incoorporating walking into your daily routine will reduce your carbon footprint and bring you a healthy habit instead of using cars to get to closer destinations. Even just walking 30 minutes or less every day will greatly improve your cardiovascular fitness, reducing body fat, and boosting muscle endurance.
