Air Pollution

What is Air Polution?

Air pollution is the contamination of the environment by any chemical, physical, or biological agent that changes the natural characteristics of the atmosphere. The primary sources of air pollution are vehicle emissions, fuel oils and natural gas to heat homes, by-products of manufacturing and power generation, coal-fueled power plants, and fumes from chemical production.

How Does Air Pollution Affect Our Environment?

Air pollution harms the environment by emitting greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide and methane, which leads to altered weather patterns and environmental disturbances. It also damages vegetation and ecosystems through the deposition of harmful pollutants like sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides, which disrupt nutrient cycles and inhibit plant growth. Air pollution can also affect people with asthma, children, or people with any other respiratory-related issues.

Solutions to Control/Minimize Air Pollution

Plant Trees

Trees play an important role in reducing air pollution. Through a process called photosynthesis, trees ingest carbon dioxide and release oxygen. Tiny pores on the tree leaf surfaces take in toxic pollutants. Planting trees can reduce the impact of air pollution, whereas deforestation worsens its effects. There are several organizations that help plant trees locally and globally.

Switching to Clean Energy

Clean sources of energy can help address air pollution and, ultimately, climate change. In Florida, solar energy is becoming more prevalent. Solar energy can be used to power entire homes through solar panels. According to the International Energy Agency (IEA), solar energy reduced CO2 emissions by 2.6 million metric tons in 2019.

Solar Energy Info
Conserve Energy

Conserve energy everywhere! From school and work to your very own home, you can make simple changes such as turning off lights or unplugging appliances when you're not using them. The less energy that is used, the less pollution in the air.
